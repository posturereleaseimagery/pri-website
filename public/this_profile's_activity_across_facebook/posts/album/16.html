This is for advanced followers. In response to a discussion on this earlier post, https://www.facebook.com/posturereleaseimagery/posts/980915478605411 , I decided to create this complex chart. It takes studying. Perhaps nothing is too obvious in it. The chart shows caricatures of the four type made eight... by showing the distinctions of the male and female versions. The creatures with the dorsal-ventral wave are the most recognizable, I am sure... but they are drawn in two, somewhat different versions, to indicate imagining the various ones with greater emphasis on the ventral surface or the dorsal surface. The other creatures are from the peristaltic wave types, in this case with an additional entirely horizontal version illustrated... in order to make them easy to compare with the dorsal-ventral wave types. What should be understood is that tonal patterns (that are experienced through imagery) can and do exist in the presence of other patterns. Each of our specific type's dorsal-ventral wave patterns exist within us along with our type's peristaltic wave type. And some predictable dorsal-ventral wave differences on each of us also are present. ALSO, there are numerous other influences on us that make their home in our flesh. It might seem, therefore, that we are hopelessly complex when seeking to untangle some of our habits. I would say, yes, we are complex... but not hopelessly so. See what you can make of thinking and imagining various qualities in the same type, one right after another. Things to note: all of the creatures are facing to the right. The horizontal peristaltic type are place above or below the male or female version of the dorsal-ventral wave types that they match. What looks like heads separated from bodies in the peristaltic types are to imagined as the whole director segment... meaning that the neck and half of the shoulder and arms are to be included in the particular tonal shape indicated.

The dogma of Posture Release Imagery is in its principles. Without knowing those principles and understanding their meaning, the imagery here is fairly dead-end. It will work but will not substantially help you over the years. It will become less sharp and distorted. Of course, those principles should be tested, not only personally but also in a broader scientific manner. Time will tell if that happens. I pray that that happens, because PRI, in my estimation, is considerably more than just imagery that works well for some and not for others. It is the beginnings of a “science of sensation”… and sensation’s direct effects on structure and movement. PRI is also among the sciences of origin. It postulates the origin of sensation in the earliest of organisms that gathered data on shape or form as the means to our own improved structures and movement. So, if Posture Release Imagery interests you, I suggest you explore it deeply, not just for immediate cessation of discomforts or a gratification of some sort. A greater understanding of your postural and movement habits as well as your own personality and that of others awaits you. Finally a deepening of spiritual or religious understanding is also possible. So, I recommend that followers, new or old, either dig deeper into PRI or give it up entirely. Skimming off of the top of things is a modern disease that at least the internet fosters. I know myself of this tendency. Feel free to write with comments or questions. I may not offer too much more on this Facebook page, but I will always respond to questions and substantial comments. John

This simple image — the Archetype Tetrapod — is a visual guide to reconnecting with our ancestral support system. This is a creature whose whole front surface rests into the ground and whose back domes gently upward into space. And even though you’re sitting in a chair, your body is still built on that same design. ⬛ The underside (in black) gives you weight, grounding, and support. ⬜ The upper surface (in white) gives you openness, lift, and gentle expansion. Those two surfaces still exist in you. And they shape your posture more than muscular effort ever will. 🐾 Try this 1–2 minute imagery practice: 1️⃣ Imagine you’re lying like the creature in the image (even while seated). Picture your belly, ribs, and the front of your neck settling into the ground beneath you. 2️⃣ Let your underside feel heavy. With each exhale, sense the whole front surface becoming warm, supported, and at ease. 3️⃣ Let your back gently rise. Allow the upper surface to widen and lift, like the creature’s pale dome expanding upward. No effort. Just a soft, natural spreading. 4️⃣ Hold both sensations together. ⬇️ Underside supported. ⬆️ Upper surface expanding. Notice how your posture shifts without forcing anything. This is one of the most direct ways to reorganize your body using imagery instead of effort. What did you feel when you tried it? 💬 We’d love to hear what changed for you.

The Droplet becomes the Body Surfaces John starts at the very beginning, not with anatomy, but with the simplest possible idea of a living organism: a single, soft droplet suspended in gravity. A membrane with an inside, an outside, and the constant pressures from an environment that shapes it. Even the earliest life forms had to orient to the planet. They had to negotiate pressure from above, pressure from within, and pressure from below. That negotiation creates a surface, a kind of primordial geodesic support system, and from that comes the earliest sense of "top" and "bottom," a dorsal and ventral relationship. PRI works from here, with the understanding that no matter how complex our bodies have become (bones, muscles, fascia, breath, behavior) we're still built on this fundamental architecture. Every body has a single surface with two different tonal qualities that manage two different forces in the same gravitational field that shaped the first organisms on Earth. The exercises in PRI are an attempt to return to this simplicity. To understand at what a life form is, before it grows limbs or a spine or a personality, and to trace how that earliest orientation still lives in our posture today.
