I found this photo among the photos of a friend of a friend on Facebook. I don’t remember any longer which friend or friend of the friend! But the photo fascinates me. Here’s why: Here is a female gorilla in the foreground and, in the background, a male. That is not too hard to see. Sexual dimorphism is pretty far along with gorillas. It is not too difficult to tell them apart. But I also see their different posture/personality inclinations. Makes me think of the posture types I outline on my blog's type-specific page ( http://posturereleaseimagery.org/posture-exercises-type-specific-imagery ) and also on this FB page (#5 - the dorsal/ventral wave, http://on.fb.me/cz8IE7 ). These two gorillas are opposite sexes and appear to be opposite "posture personalities" ... as I would argue is predominantly the case in human heterosexual pairing. Of course a couple can have similar postures and behaviors over time, but I would argue that one of the pair is copying or tacitly asceding to the style of the other (in appearance). Of course I also don't know if these two gorillas are a pair or pals or what, but what I see still interests me. It is in the carriage of the head and neck, the shoulders and arms and back. Of course, this is just a moment in time, but I am sure that they are living out their habits here... for all (other gorillas) to see.
