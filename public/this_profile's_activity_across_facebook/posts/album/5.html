This image illustrates how we can come to feel more grounded with the basic dorsal and ventral relationship. As I repeat (perhaps ad nauseum) the dorsal surface is healthiest when it feels light and virtually floating but the ventral surface is generally healthiest when it is sensed as condensed or gently contracted. An additional sensation healthy to experience and imagine on the ventral surface (as shown by the first and last archetypal caricatures here) is to feel fully (ventrally) in contact with the ground. Imagining that your entire ventral surface is in contact with the earth (even though we are upright) gives a sense of sturdiness that we should more often have. This sturdiness comes from changed and more appropriate use of our postural muscles. The entire ventral surface musculature becomes engaged to share the job of muscularly supporting / balancing us on whatever ventral portions of our body are actually (not imaginarily) in contact with the ground. We generally think of ourselves as the delicately "balanced" evolved creature shown in the middle figure, but that very thought can make us less available to a sense and reality of sturdiness we should have.
