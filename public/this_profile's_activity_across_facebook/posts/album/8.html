I think these creatures are cute... but their function here is more to illustrate how tonal patterns in the body create waves. On the human body it is more complex to represent these waves (but I try, in other illustrations). You may be able to notice how the waves, one form or another somewhat frozen in each of us, suggest our tendencies, our structural or "pre-movement" tendencies. Can you see one who may tend to be cautious, focused and assertive, fearless, laid back? Do you have a tendency that you wish was not so strong? Can you see how the patterns of muscle tonus predictably affect the focus of the eyes and the quality of the arms and legs? Read, study, and see how imagining your "opposite" in dorsal/ventral wave stye (and other type-specific imagery) can free you up psychophysically... "psycho" and "physically."

This chart illustrates qualitative differences between the four dorsal-ventral wave types. Each type has a column that of illustrations I will describe and to the far left, outside the boxes, is a column showing some of the "neutral" models used to derived the variations in the others. The top row shows wiggly caricatures that suggest the sort of frozen structure pattern shown more human-like in the second row. The third row are horizontal four-legged varieties you are now familiar with. The last row represents a way of imaging all of the types as well as the neutral version. Practice with all the type imagery makes it easier to honestly experience the "neutral" version... though it is not a goal to become a different type than you are... the goal is to become less extreme in the type you are. We are all one of the types and imagining our "opposite" is the most effective way to discover the grace and ease of the "middle (and released) way" or more neutral version. In the image in the last row, for initial ease, imagine that you are wearing an oversized hat that fits well over your head and that you are wearing a large costume that billows and is snug oon the remainder of the body in the ways shown.
