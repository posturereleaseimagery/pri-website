The Droplet becomes the Body Surfaces John starts at the very beginning, not with anatomy, but with the simplest possible idea of a living organism: a single, soft droplet suspended in gravity. A membrane with an inside, an outside, and the constant pressures from an environment that shapes it. Even the earliest life forms had to orient to the planet. They had to negotiate pressure from above, pressure from within, and pressure from below. That negotiation creates a surface, a kind of primordial geodesic support system, and from that comes the earliest sense of "top" and "bottom," a dorsal and ventral relationship. PRI works from here, with the understanding that no matter how complex our bodies have become (bones, muscles, fascia, breath, behavior) we're still built on this fundamental architecture. Every body has a single surface with two different tonal qualities that manage two different forces in the same gravitational field that shaped the first organisms on Earth. The exercises in PRI are an attempt to return to this simplicity. To understand at what a life form is, before it grows limbs or a spine or a personality, and to trace how that earliest orientation still lives in our posture today.

Recently, I introduced to AT Global a Posture Release Imagery principle that addresses the dorsal/ventral relationship in relation to AT’s “forward and up.” This time, and for a time or two more, I will introduce additional aspects of the relationship and more imagery to access its benefits. (The dorsal and ventral surfaces are generally poorly described in drawings by others. Having a fairly precise sense of what is dorsal and what is ventral on our bodies is important to success with PRI imagery. You will notice that more than half of the body here is shown as dorsal… and should be.) This first illustration here shows land-bound creatures from the simplest creature that has a top and a bottom (and “prefers” upright) and has a front and a back (and predominantly moves forward). It rests ventrally on the earth. Then come the creatures “on all fours” who can support themselves further from the ground. Finally, there is us, who stand and walk while having a portion of our carriage “on edge.” (That segment of the body I call our “motor segment.” I will discuss the importance of identifying our “segments” later down the road). I have drawn the creatures here with white dorsal surfaces and black or dark ventral surfaces. That is important in the imagery to follow. When doing the imagery exercise that follows here, endeavor to imagine/think of the white surfaces as not only white but also light and fluffy and expanding. Conversely, imagine the dark or black surfaces as also heavy, dense, and contracting. Work to develop a good sense on your body of what is dorsal and what is from these first two illustrations. (Our entire body surface is either dorsal or ventral with a small area I call the dorsal/ventral seam). Next proceed to the third illustration, which is the exercise. Proceeding from the top illustration here, spend some time imagining you have a body surface that is just the opposite of what I have been talking about. I doubt you will want to spend too long imagining this version of yourself… and I don’t blame you. The next illustration down is like what has been recommended, and if you imagine ALL of your dorsal surface as white, fluffy, light, and expanding while imaging ALL of your ventral surface as dark, dense, and contracting, you will be much happier and sense a much better Use. The last two illustrations are intensifiers of what you just imagined and can provide greater insights into a habit structure you may carry about too often. Notice in the next illustration your ventral surface has full contact and support from the earth, like the earliest of organisms on land enjoyed. In fact, since the ventral surface is imagined contracting, it can be thought of as not only resting on the earth but also embracing it. Remember, there is nothing to do here except imagine. The final illustration suggests that your ventral surface has become the earth and your dorsal surface has expanded to fill everywhere above. Don’t expect success yet with this last version of this exercise at this point. But maybe you will surprise me.
